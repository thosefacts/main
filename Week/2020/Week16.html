#Weekly News

Archived: Week 16

13 April

Monday

Japan divided over lockdown measures Japan's PM Shinzo Abe has recently been receiving backlash over his dealing with the Corona crisis. Some Japanese find the PM's countermeasures too nonchalant and fear that corona infections will skyrocket soon | Yesterday, people criticized Abe for twittering a photo in which he seemed 'too relaxed' while others were 'fighting for their lives' | Today, Tokyo's governor delivered a speech calling on people to stay at home - in clash with Abe's less urging policy.

Boris Johnson released from hospital after Corona illness The Prime Minister of Great Brittain was treated at intensive care since April 6. After being released today, he thanked the the hopital's staff for saving his life | Johnson is the first world leader known to have been infected with the Corona virus.

Chinese government has banned sales of Animal Crossing game Animal crossing is an rpg-game allowing players to socially interact in an imaginative world. It recently begot much popularity worldwide | China banned sales after noticing Hong Kongese used the game to continue protests against the Chinese government | To note, it is somewhat common for China to ban the sale of specific games.

Several European countries will loosen Lockdown A set of European countries will slowly re-open certain industries tomorrow | The relieve comes in reaction to a stagnation of newly infected Corona patients | France has however prolonged its rather tight lockdown to 11 May.

Intense combat near Tripoli Libya's national forces (of the Government of National Accord) experienced intense clashes with the Independence army led by Khalifa Haftar. They seized control of two strategically significant towns close to Tripoli | The control may help prevent further power and water cuts ~ which caused problems last week | The Government of National Accord (internationally recognized as government) has fought Haftar's Libyan National Army (LNA) since 2014.

Tornado caused 34 casualties along Southern states (USA). A storm swept across multiple American states. 34 people lost their lives due to the storm | Many US households lost power and some were damaged or destroyed.

14 April

Tuesday

Turkey releases 90K prisoners around 45K prisoners will be temporarily released until May. Another 45K are granted permanent leave | The law controversially excludes Gulen supporters - journalists and politicians who president Erdogan accuses of terrorism / a coup attempt

Trump states that the U.S. economy will soon be re-opened. Corona has killed in excess of 23,000 Americans, but Trump's administration noticed how the numbers are starting to plateau | Trump recently struggled to maintain control of some states that sought to preliminarily open their economies despite the federal policy. At yesterday's briefing he responded with "The president of the United States calls the shots".

Europeans calling for green restart of economy Several influential Europeans are calling upon the EU to restart economies in an environment-friendly way | Yesterday, Japan's Environment Minister voiced similar concerns.

Barack Obama endorses Joe Biden's candidacy Barack Obama was president from 2009-2017. Joe Biden was his Vice President at that time. Obama shared the video via his Twitter | Bernie Sanders recently endorsed Biden as well.

G7 teleconference scheduled for Thursday 16 G7 members will discuss their approach to the ongoing pandemic | Leaders of all 7 member-states (FRA, GB, USA, ITA, CAN, JAP and GER) will convene.

US military assumes that Covid-19 has natural origins The US military presented its belief that Covid-19 was not artificially made in a (Chinese) lab | There have been some concerns that the virus was developed to strengthen China's international position | While the evidence suggests a natural origin, the military's belief remains inconclusive.

15 April

Wednesday

Trump announced the US would cut funding to the WHO Last week Trump had already threatened to cut funding due to the WHO's inssufficient reaction to the Corona pandemic | The US was the biggest contributor to the WHO, making up roughly 15% of the organization's budget | The Director-General of the WHO had recently voiced confidence that the US would retain its funding to the organization.

Social media woes China for causing droughts along
Mekong river A report which was published on Frirday (10th of April) indicated that China's structure of dams caused droughts downstream. While water volumes were superfluous in Chinese parts of the Mekong river, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam and Laos dealt with record low levels of water | Social media has caught on to the report, and #StopMekongDam is now trending in Indochinese countries | Activist groups are urging China to be more cooperative and transparant

Chinese official held speech about Hong Kong Shortly after making office, the head of the Hong Kong liaison office Luo Huining delivered a speech urging the authorities to pass a national security legislation law 'as soon as possible'. The law directs itself mostly to Hong Kong, which had remained rather independent of China | A law of similar nature (known as Article 23) was met with massive demonstrations in Hong Kong last year, because the citizens saw China's efforts as treacherous: Hong Kong conjoined with mainland China in 1997 under the condition of 'one country, two systems'.

German authorities arrested 4 figures suspected of planning an act of terrorism The arrested group has affiliation with Islamic State and had probably been preparing an attack on one particular target who made public statements they deem Islamophobic.

16 April

Thursday

WW2 veteran raises millions for Corona charity A British WW2 veteran started a fundraiser, hoping to raise 1000 pounds by walking back and forth on live video. In his hopes to relieve those struct by Corona, he had never expected to raise millions of pounds (over £17.7M / $22M)

G7 agrees on policy In a conference call today, G7 leaders have agreed upon reviewing and reforming the WHO. They concluded that, regarding the response to Corona, the WHO failed in terms of transparancy and proper management.

The UN warns many children could die in recession The UN voiced concerns that many children could die due to a Corona-related recession. They predict that impoverished children are at increased risk of dying to food- or water deprivation | Secretary General Gutteres encourage countries to take action so as to mitigate these risks.

Russia tested its anti-satellite missile Russia tested its anti-satellite missile on Wednesday | The USA presented the news and added that space has become (and will remain) a significant theatre of war.

Japan has expanded its state of emergency Prime Minister Shinzo Abe has extended Japan's lockdown measures in hopes to settle down public outrage against his reaction to the Corona virus | Besides the PM's official dealings with Corona, many Japanese criticize more personal affairs | Abe's wife visit to a public shrine (April 15th) had amplified the woes on social media against Shinzo Abe | Earlier, there was upheaval about the PM's twitter post in which he looked 'too relaxed'.

Prisoner exchange concerning Ukraine and pro-Russian separatists The Ukrainian national government and pro-Russian separatists have agreed to exchange prisoners. It is unclear how many separatists have been released, but Ukraine's government shared it is happy to receive 19 of its citizens | The prisoners could be called POWs as their incarceration links to the civil war that started in 2014 - which relates to Russia's annexation of Crimea.

USA claims the Iranian navy provoked theirs in Persian Gulf USA claims 11 Iranian vessels approached 6 of their own in the Persian gulf | A US-sailor video recorded how Iranian ships disobeyed rules of conduct at sea, such as crossing the stern. | The US navy reported that, besides provocative manoevering, Iranian ships came within 10 yards (9.5 meters) of one of their vessels.

Foreign states spy on USA's research on Corona treatment Tonya Ugoretz, a senior member of the FBI, revealed that foreign countries made attempts to spy on US-based institutions that research treatments against the Corona virus | she made her statements in an online discussion of think tank the Aspen Institute

17 April

Friday

USA wants China to join future arms control talks Secretary of State Mike Pompeo informed Russia that the USA wishes China to join arms control talks in the future | While China has a smaller article than Russia and USA do, they are actively improving its quality in a modernization program.

Chinese ship stirs in Malaysian terriotrial waters A Chinese survey vessel, Haiyang Dizhi 8 has caused stirrings in Malaysia's territorial waters. The transgression comes amid controversy over China's activity in the South China Sea; the USA voiced its suspicions that China sought to expand its presence in the maritime area | The concerning survey vessel is known for having caused problems in the past and is currently being tailed by Malaysian Coastguard vessels.

Myanmar releases 25K prisoners The official pardon is meant to mark Myanmar's traditional New Year | Turkey released 90K prisoners on tuesday.

Iran calls USA's presence in Persian Gulf provocative Yesterday the USA claimed 11 Iranian vessels harassed their convoy in the Persian Gulf. Today, the Iranian minister of defense responded saying how the USA's claims are 'baseless'. He added that the US Navy's presence in the Persian Gulf is aggressive and harasses Iran.

China promises it has not covered up elements of the Corona outbreak Yesterday, the US president said his country will investigate the origin of the Corona virus; whether it was artificially made in a Chinese lab. Trump also said that the WHO and China likely withheld information and could have informed the world about Covid-19's existence weeks before than they did. China's foreign minister responded, saying that China has not covered up any elements of the Corona outbreak | China controversially increased their Corona death toll by a sheer 50% for the city of Wuhan, adding to doubts about China's accuracy in reporting information.

US workers demonstrate for having to working under exposure to Corona A group of factory workers is currently demonstrating over having to work under 'contagion hazards'. They are worried about several workers dying to the Corona virus - and that they will be next. The demonstrations arose in Mexican border towns, but has sparked national debate over which industries should remain open during the Corona pandemic.
