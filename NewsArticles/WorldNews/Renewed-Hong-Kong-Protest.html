Demonstration in Hong Kong

27 April 2020

The first protest since Hong Kong's lockdown

   For the first time since Hong Kong's Covid-related lockdown, citizens have restarted demonstrating against undesired unfluence from China's government.

   The group was reportedly singing and attempting to perform a peaceful and fully legal protest. Reuters reported that people shouted "Liberate Hong Kong, revolution of our times!".

The arrest of 15 activists

   What might have triggered the demonstration, is the recent arrest of 15 high-profile protesters and official statements that the activists' charges would not be dropped..

   Hong Kong police eventually showed up at the demonstration, with shields and other tools made for dispersing protests. Despite these efforts being successful, the police ordered several stores to close momentarily.

Background information

  The original cause for massive upheaval among Hong Kong's population was the implemenation of Article 23 in 2019. The concerning law enabled the extradition of Hong Kong prisoners and was met with strong resentment since Hongkongers saw it as a Chinese attempt to upheave their unique sovereign status within China. The protesting Hongkongers wish to retain the ‘one country, two systems’ condition, granting Hong Kong a certain degree of sovereignty within China.
