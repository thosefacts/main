Germany Compels its citizens to wear facial masks

27 April 2020

Most German states oblige citizens to wear facial masks in public

   Most German states have implemented a law that obliges people to wear facial masks in public. Most implemented the law on Monday (27 April) and have placed rather high fines on violations.

  Since proper masks are rather scarce, the law condones the use of a cloth or scarf as facial cover.

Potential relation with demonstrations

  On Sunday (26 April), thousands of Berliners protested against the lockdown. They claimed that the Corona-virus as the government suggests; they are against the government's lockdown of the state. They called for 'regaining freedom' and yelled statements such as 'I want my life back'. The rules for public appearance that were implemented this week may have been triggered by that sizeable demonstration on The Rosa Luxemburg Platz.

   One could the measures as the governments reaction to this weekend's demonstration as they are restricting people's ability to do as they please further amid times that the country is slowly uplifting the lockdown.
